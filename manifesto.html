BEAUTY IN DECAY

01. PRESERVATION IS A LIE

We spend our lives trying to back up our data, embalm our dead, and archive our memories. It is a pathetic rebellion against the natural order. Everything rusts. When you accept that the servers will one day burn, the anxiety stops. The broken link (Error 404) is not a failure. It is the internet breathing out.

02. THE AESTHETICS OF ROT

Look closely at a glitch. Look at the jagged artifacts of a corrupted JPEG. Look at code that has broken and spilled its guts across the screen. There is more truth in that chaos than in a cleanly designed, corporate webpage.

To witness decay is to witness transformation. We do not mourn the dead flesh; we marvel at the ecosystem of the maggots.

03. THE DIGITAL CORPSE

You leave pieces of yourself all over the web. Passwords, abandoned social media accounts, forgotten emails. You are building a digital corpse. Long after your physical heart stops, your ghost will still be pinging servers in California and Virginia. Let it rot. Let it become part of the noise.
